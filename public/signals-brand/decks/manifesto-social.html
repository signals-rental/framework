The Signals Rental
Manifesto
10 Principles for Open-Source
Rental Management
Rental businesses deserve tools that are powerful, transparent, and truly theirs.
signals.rent
Signals
01
Free Forever
MIT-licensed permanently. No “open core” catch. No features held back. Production-ready software you can run, modify, and distribute without restriction.
01 / 10
Signals
02
One Version
for Everyone
No “community edition” and “enterprise edition.” Every rental business runs the same code. The difference is what you build on top.
02 / 10
Signals
03
Built for Rental
Purpose-built from the ground up. Availability, scheduling, maintenance, kit prep, dry hire, wet hire, sub-hire, cross-hire. Foundation, not afterthought.
03 / 10
Signals
04
Self-Host Without
Compromise
Your server. Your terms. Your data. Self-hosting is a first-class experience, not a workaround.
04 / 10
Signals
05
API-First,
Plugin-Native
Every capability accessible programmatically. Plugin architecture by design. Build on it, extend it, sell on top of it.
05 / 10
Signals
06
Commercial Without
Compromise
Open source doesn’t mean anti-business. The Framework stays free. The ecosystem around it is open for business.
06 / 10
Signals
07
Community-Shaped
Shaped by the people who use it — operators, warehouse staff, hire desks, technicians, developers. Decisions made in the open.
07 / 10
Signals
08
Opinionated
but Not Rigid
Strong defaults for real-world rental. Deep configuration, clean overrides. Change behaviour without forking the core.
08 / 10
Signals
09
Quality Over Velocity
We ship when it’s ready. A stable release means stable. Trust is earned by not breaking things.
09 / 10
Signals
10
Rental is a
Community
The hire industry runs on relationships. Lending kit, covering shortfalls, sharing knowledge. We share what we build.
10 / 10

Section 04 — Typography Rules
BRD-008d Typography Rules REV A

Typography Rules

Consistent type usage reinforces the Signals technical identity.

Do
Heading in Chakra Petch Body text in Martian Mono at 13px for technical precision.
Chakra Petch 700 for headings. Martian Mono 400/500 for body and technical text.
Don't
Heading in Comic Sans Body text in Georgia ruins the technical feel entirely.
Never substitute unauthorized typefaces. No system fonts, no serif fonts for body copy.
Do
Uppercase Headings -0.01em tracking
Headings are always uppercase with tight -0.01em letter-spacing for a confident, engineered feel.
Don't
lowercase headings +0.3em tracking — too wide
Never use lowercase headings or excessive letter-spacing. It contradicts the brand's precision.
Do
[REF: BRD-008D] Technical Annotation Scale: 1:1 — Units: PX
Technical annotations use 9px Martian Mono, uppercase, with 0.08em tracking.
Don't
Using Chakra Petch at 13px for body text defeats the purpose of having a monospace body font. The display typeface should be reserved for headings and the brand mark only.
Never use the display font for body text. Chakra Petch is for headings and the mark only.
Section 05 — Co-Branding
BRD-008e Co-Branding REV A

Co-Branding Rules

Guidelines for presenting the Signals mark alongside partner brands.

1
Signals mark always appears on the left
In any co-branded lockup, the Signals mark takes the left position to establish hierarchy.
Signals × Partner
2
Minimum 2x clear space between marks
Maintain at least 2x the clear-space unit between the Signals mark and the partner mark.
Signals
2x
Partner
3
Partner logos should match visual weight
Scale partner logos so they appear optically balanced alongside the Signals mark. Neither brand should dominate.
Signals × Partner
4
Never combine more than two brands in a single lockup
Multi-brand lockups create visual clutter. If three or more brands are involved, list them separately.
Max 2 brands per lockup
5
The corner accent must always be present — never remove it
The corner accent device is the most distinctive element of the Signals identity. Removing it for co-branding or any other reason is never permitted. It persists in all contexts, including monograms.
